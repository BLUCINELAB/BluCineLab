BLUCINE / STUDIO / METHOD

STUDIO

STUDIO

STRUCTURE, ATMOSPHERE, DIRECTION

Blucine works across web design, visual identity, spatial construction and image direction.

The aim is not to decorate a project, but to organise how it is perceived, read and remembered.

FIELDS

PRIMARY WORKING AREAS

Four operative territories, treated as connected systems rather than separate services.

01

WEB DESIGN

Editorial websites and front-end systems built through hierarchy, pacing, tonal control and readable structure.

02

VISUAL IDENTITY

Identity systems, graphic language and visual continuity for brands, institutions and selective contexts.

03

EXHIBITION AND INSTALLATION

Spatial environments, temporary structures and display logic built through scale, rhythm and atmosphere.

04

BRAND AND IMAGE DIRECTION

Visual systems, tonal continuity and directional logic for campaigns, audiovisual projects and international-facing work.

METHOD

THREE CONSTANTS

The method stays compact: reduce, align, intensify.

SELECTION

REMOVE WHAT WEAKENS THE READ

The first move is usually subtractive: clarify the hierarchy, reduce dispersion, keep only what carries tension and function.

ATMOSPHERE

BUILD THE PERCEPTIVE FIELD

Light, type, spacing, surface and image are treated as one continuous field, not as disconnected layers.

COHERENCE

KEEP ONE INTERNAL LANGUAGE

The project should remain recognisable across page, image, space and direction without losing precision.

PROCESS

LET THE WORKING STATE REMAIN INTELLIGIBLE

Research, tests and fragments are not waste. They are part of the project’s internal construction and often its clearest evidence.

POSITION

WHAT FITS BEST

Projects that need visual order, atmosphere and a durable system.

CONTEXT

CULTURAL, EDITORIAL AND SELECTIVE COMMERCIAL WORK

The studio fits contexts where visual tone matters, but where tone must be sustained by structure and not by effects alone.

APPROACH

LESS ACCUMULATION, MORE PRECISION

Fewer arbitrary gestures, stronger hierarchy, better spacing, more controlled presence across every visible layer.
